Diafilm

Diafilm — (from Russian Диафильм, meaning Film Strip).

I was too young to catch that era. But once, at my grandmother's, we found an old diafilm projector. We tried to make it work but its time had clearly passed.

This tool is my way of finishing that afternoon.

Part of the Visual Dep. of SAM.

Hestikon — 2026

Technical Passport

Grain: Fixed monochromatic noise - always present, not configurable. Soviet film stock wasn't smooth. Neither is this.

Warm Tone: Shifts colour temperature toward amber. Mimics the chemical drift of ageing emulsion.

Scratches & Dust: Procedural vertical scratches and dust particles. Generated fresh each time.

Vignette: Soft darkening at the edges, light pooling in the centre.

Frame & Perforations: The characteristic diafilm border with sprocket holes. Fixed. Always there.

Caption: Text rendered at the bottom of the frame, in the diafilm tradition.

Smart Optimization: Same weight reduction as Zerno. The past doesn't have to be heavy.

AI Ethics & Curation: The technical heavy lifting was done by AI. I decided what it should do and how it should feel.
